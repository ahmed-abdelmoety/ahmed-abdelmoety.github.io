About

I am a Master of Mechanics student at Technische Universität Darmstadt, concurrently holding two research assistant positions — one in experimental plasticity and ductile fracture characterization at the Fachgebiet Werkstoffmechanik, and the other developing an automated documentation process for BoSSS, an open-source discontinuous Galerkin CFD framework at the Fachgebiet Strömungsdynamik.

At Werkstoffmechanik, my work is focused on characterizing ductile fracture and plasticity by analysing experimental data from multi-axial loading tests, contributing to the development of a new constitutive model for ductile materials, and tracking the crack growth rate using FEM for different materials (Ductile materials and ICE). On the other hand, the scope of my research position at FDY is to develop a fully automated documentation process that makes large-scale scientific documentation reproducible and verifiable.

Prior to TU Darmstadt, I completed a Bachelor of Mechanical Engineering at UCSI University, Malaysia, and earned a scholarship for academic excellence throughout my studies. My bachelor's thesis on U-tube heat exchanger optimization was published as a first-authored paper in a Q1 Scopus journal (Impact Factor 5.4), and was awarded Best Bachelor Thesis in the department.
