About

I'm a Master of Mechanics student at TU Darmstadt, where I also work as a research assistant in two concurrent positions — one focused on experimental plasticity and fracture characterization, and one on building computational tooling for an open-source CFD framework.

My academic journey started in Malaysia at UCSI University, where I graduated with First Class Honors and a scholarship for academic excellence. My bachelor's thesis on heat exchanger optimization became a Q1 published paper.

I'm driven by the idea that good engineering is both precise and creative — whether that's deriving a constitutive model, automating a documentation pipeline with Python, or designing a clear diagram that makes a complex concept instantly understandable.

Open to PhD opportunities, research collaborations, and industry roles in computational simulation and structural analysis.
